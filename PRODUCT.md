# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Family and friends sharing a Portuguese-speaking household. Used in a practical, real-world moment: standing in the kitchen with no idea what to cook, wanting an answer fast. Not power users, not food enthusiasts in search of inspiration — just people who want the decision made for them with minimal effort.

## Product Purpose

pickmeal exists to end "what do I cook?" paralysis. The primary job: take a few quick inputs and produce a recipe worth making tonight. Success is getting from app-open to committed recipe in under 30 seconds with no cognitive strain.

## Brand Personality

Direct, quiet, useful. Gets out of the way and lets the answer appear. Confident without being loud. The interface should feel like it already knows what you need — it just needed a moment to think.

## Anti-references

- SaaS startup aesthetics: gradient hero sections, metric dashboards, neon accents, "product-led growth" energy
- Heavy food blog / Pinterest: cluttered layouts, recipe cards stacked to infinity, engagement-bait presentation
- Delivery app urgency: aggressive primary CTAs, countdown timers, promotional noise
- Clinical diet trackers: white-and-blue sterility, calorie obsession, medicalized tone
- Anything that makes the user think harder than the problem requires

## Design Principles

1. **One job, done well.** The app has a single purpose. Every element either serves that purpose or should not exist.
2. **Zero decision overhead.** Smart defaults pre-select sensible options. The user adjusts only what matters to them tonight.
3. **The recipe is the product.** The UI is a doorway, not the destination. Once a recipe appears, the interface should feel like it stepped aside.
4. **Speed to answer.** Fewest taps possible from open to committed recipe. No confirmation steps, no progress bars that pad time, no unnecessary transitions.
5. **Restraint as trust.** A calm, quiet interface communicates confidence. Noise and decoration signal insecurity. When in doubt, remove.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Touch targets minimum 44px (critical for mobile kitchen use with wet or distracted hands). Sufficient contrast on parchment background. Semantic HTML for screen readers. No reliance on color alone to communicate state.
